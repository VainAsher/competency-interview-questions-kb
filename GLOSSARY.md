# Glossary

Shared terms for the knowledge base. Only the orchestrator edits this file;
workers return new terms in their JSON reply. `Source key` refers to
`SOURCE_REGISTRY.md` (`-` = general usage or knowledge-base editorial term).

| Term | Definition | Source key |
|------|------------|------------|
| Adverse impact (four-fifths rule) | A selection method has adverse impact where a minority group's pass rate falls below 80 per cent (four-fifths) of the majority group's. A common detection heuristic which College of Policing guidance notes has no legal footing in the UK. | cop-vbr-selection-2018 |
| Anchor (EPSO) | An observable element of a general competency in the EPSO Competency Framework (2022), used by assessors to recognise and score the competency. | epso-cf-leaflet |
| Arithmetic decision making | Ranking candidates by aggregated assessment scores, optionally with thresholds on key competencies. College of Policing guidance states it is the most valid decision method, in contrast to 'rational' decision making where assessors discuss performances and decide together. | cop-vbr-selection-2018 |
| Assessment centre | A selection event (in person or virtual) combining several tasks — typically an interview plus presentations, group exercises, written analyses or in-tray/e-tray simulations — observed by multiple assessors. | cipd-selection-methods |
| Assessment centre (EPSO sense) | The final testing phase of a pre-2023 EPSO open competition, assessing candidates individually and in groups across several exercises against defined competencies. Abolished by the Management Board decision of 31 January 2023. | epso-ac-brochure-ad244 |
| Asynchronous (pre-recorded) video interview | An interview with no live interviewer: questions are presented on screen and the candidate records timed answers on a platform for later review. Also called on-demand or recorded video interview. | csc-assessments |
| Automated decision-making (ADM) | As used by the ICO in a recruitment context, decisions based solely on automated processing of personal information that produce legal or similarly significant effects on individuals. | ico-recruitment-rewired-2026 |
| Behaviour (Success Profiles) | In the UK Civil Service Success Profiles framework, "the actions and activities that people do which result in effective performance in a job". | govuk-sp-behaviours |
| Benchmark (Success Profiles scoring) | A minimum score, per element or in total, that a recruiting panel sets for progression or appointment where there is a job and business need (e.g. 5 on lead behaviours, or 20 across several behaviours). | copfs-assessment |
| Briefing exercise | An exercise in which the candidate takes a role, receives preparation material and responds verbally to a structured question set; the College of Policing runs a 12-question, three-part national version with new information injected at each part. | cop-oap-candidate-guidance-2024 |
| Calibration (frame-of-reference training) | Pre-live alignment of multiple assessors on what each point of a rating scale means, for example by running a mock assessment or agreeing expected performance levels before the first live candidate. | cop-vbr-selection-2018 |
| Capability-Based Interview (Ireland) | A PAS structured interview building a profile across capability dimensions and sub-dimensions — skills, strengths, knowledge, values, motivation, interests — beyond past behaviour alone. | pj-interview-advice |
| CAR | Context, Action, Results — a three-part compression of STAR taught by the University of Cambridge Careers Service. | cambridge-interviews |
| CARL | Context, Action, Results, Learning — a reflective four-part answer structure documented in the University of Edinburgh Reflection Toolkit as an adaptation of a job interview technique. | edinburgh-carl |
| Case study (EPSO) | A written test based on a scenario in which the candidate solves or reacts to problems using only the material provided; indicative pre-2023 duration 1h30. | eurlex-epso-general-rules-2015 |
| CAST | EPSO's permanently open contract-staff selection: applicants register in a database, are shortlisted by recruiting services, sit reasoning and competency MCQ tests, then attend interviews organised by the hiring service. | epso-contract-staff |
| CBI (EPSO) | Competency-based interview; EPSO's past-behaviour interview run at about 10 minutes per competency. Abolished for EPSO competitions by the 2023 competition model. | epso-cbi |
| Civil Service Behaviours | The nine defined behaviours of Success Profiles (Seeing the Big Picture through Delivering at Pace), each with example statements at six grade bands from AA/AO to Director/Director General. | govuk-sp-behaviours |
| Civil Service Capability Framework (Ireland) | The Irish Civil Service framework launched February 2024: seven capability dimensions (behaviours, skills, strengths, knowledge, values, motivation, interests) under four Capabilities, for grades Clerical Officer to Principal Officer. Coexists with the legacy competency model. | pj-capability-overview |
| Civil Service Code values | The four core values binding UK civil servants: integrity (putting the obligations of public service above your own personal interests), honesty (being truthful and open), objectivity (basing advice and decisions on rigorous analysis of the evidence) and impartiality (acting solely according to the merits of the case and serving equally well governments of different political persuasions). | govuk-civil-service-code |
| Civil Service Competency Model (Ireland) | The legacy per-grade competency dictionary of the Irish Civil Service, each competency expanded into effective performance indicators; still used for some roles alongside the 2024 Capability Framework. | pj-eo-competency-legacy |
| Commission for Public Service Appointments (CPSA) | The statutory regulator of Irish public-service recruitment, created by the Public Service Management (Recruitment and Appointments) Act 2004; its Code of Practice sets six recruitment principles. | cpsa-code-2022 |
| Communicating and Influencing | The UK Civil Service Success Profiles behaviour: 'Communicate purpose and direction with clarity, integrity and enthusiasm. Respect the needs, responses and opinions of others.' Treated as a single behaviour, not two. | govuk-sp-behaviours |
| Communication (EPSO) | EPSO general competency: 'Conveys information and opinions clearly, generates buy-in by facilitating interaction and engaging effectively with others.' Influencing is subsumed within it rather than assessed separately. | epso-cf-leaflet |
| Competency and Values Framework (CVF) | The College of Policing framework (2016, updated 2024): six competencies at three levels, underpinned by core values drawn from the Code of Ethics; basis of national police recruitment, promotion and development processes. | cop-cvf-guidance-2024 |
| Competency family | This knowledge base's editorial term for a cluster of closely related competencies that recur across frameworks under different names; not an official framework term. | - |
| Competency passport | The per-competency qualitative feedback document issued by an EPSO Selection Board under the pre-2023 model and made available to recruiting services alongside the reserve list. | eurlex-epso-ad-389-21 |
| Competency-based interview | A structured interview in which candidates evidence defined competencies/behaviours with examples from past experience, scored against set criteria. | - |
| Conflict of interest (EU staff) | Under Article 11a of the EU Staff Regulations, a matter in which an official has 'directly or indirectly, any personal interest such as to impair his independence, and, in particular, family and financial interests'; the official must immediately inform the Appointing Authority. | eurlex-staff-regulations |
| Credibility interval | The band (conventionally 10th-90th percentiles, an '80% credibility interval') within which the true validity is expected to fall across settings; a statement about real between-study variation, not just sampling error. | sackett-2023-iop |
| CVF level | One of three levels of increasing behavioural complexity applied to each College of Policing CVF competency. Levels are not assigned to specific ranks, though they broadly relate to practitioners (1), supervisors/managers (2) and senior managers/executive (3). | cop-cvf-guidance-2024 |
| Decision-making and getting results | EPSO general competency (2023 framework): 'Takes decisions in a shifting environment, translates them into action and works towards tangible results.' | epso-cf-leaflet |
| Delivering at Pace | UK Civil Service Success Profiles behaviour: 'Take responsibility for delivering timely and quality results with focus and drive.' Levelled by grade from organising own work (AA/AO) to driving a performance culture (Director/DG). | govuk-sp-behaviours |
| Disability Confident | Voluntary UK government scheme (three levels) whose Level 1 commitments include offering an interview to disabled people and providing reasonable adjustments. | govuk-disability-confident-signup |
| EPSO | European Personnel Selection Office — the interinstitutional office that organises staff selection procedures for the EU institutions and bodies; careers portal eu-careers.europa.eu. | epso-cf-page |
| Ethical dilemma (interview sense) | A situation in which two defensible obligations conflict, so any course of action sacrifices something legitimate. Distinguished in this knowledge base from a compliance test, where one option is simply wrong. | - |
| Ethical policing principles | The three principles of the College of Policing 2024 Code of Ethics - courage, respect and empathy, and public service - mapping onto the three core values of the 2024 CVF, under the strapline 'Doing the right things, in the right way, for the right reasons'. | cop-code-of-ethics-poster-2024 |
| EUFTE | Free-Text Essay on EU Matters — EPSO's written test assessing written communication only; explicitly neither a language test nor a test of factual knowledge, marked against five published anchors. | epso-tests-specialists-ad6-ad9 |
| Evidence bank | A prepared, reusable set of real examples from work, study, volunteering or life, each written up in STAR form and mapped to the competencies of a target framework. Knowledge-base term for a practice documented across official and careers-service guidance. | - |
| Evidence-balance scoring | Scoring in which an assessor weighs positive evidence against negative evidence rather than forming an overall impression; published examples include the COPFS 1-7 Success Profiles scale and the 1-5 competency evaluation scheme reproduced from Civil Service Resourcing guidance. | copfs-assessment |
| Exercise-specific assessor guidance | A document translating generic competency indicators into observable, exercise-specific evidence statements, produced by piloting the exercise with existing good performers. Guidance for assessors, explicitly not a model answer. | cop-vbr-selection-2018 |
| FRSTQ | Field-Related Short Text Questionnaire — EPSO's written test for secretaries (AST/SC): 10 short-text questions requiring concise, experience-based answers in 60 minutes. | epso-tests-secretaries |
| Function group (contract agents) | One of four EU contract-agent job levels (FG I manual/administrative support; FG II clerical/secretarial; FG III executive/technical; FG IV administrative/advisory/linguistic). | epso-faq-contract-agents |
| Grade band (Success Profiles) | The published groupings against which each Civil Service behaviour is separately described: AA/AO; EO; HEO/SEO; Grade 7/Grade 6; Deputy Director; Director/Director General. | govuk-sp-behaviours |
| Group exercise (EPSO) | An exercise in which candidates process information individually then reach a collective decision while assessors observe without participating; indicative duration 60 minutes. Present in the 2015 general rules, absent from 2022 notices. | eurlex-epso-general-rules-2015 |
| HSC values (Northern Ireland) | The four shared Health and Social Care values used in NI HSC recruitment: Working Together, Excellence, Openness and Honesty, Compassion. | hscni-values |
| In-tray / e-tray exercise | A simulation of a role's administrative flow — emails, memos, reports, messages to read, prioritise, action and justify within a time limit. The same exercise, paper-based versus computer-based. | lse-etray-intray |
| Influencing without authority | This knowledge base's editorial term for persuading someone who does not report to you and can decline. Not a framework term; the distinction separating the communication family from leadership. | - |
| Intrapreneurship (EPSO) | General competency in EPSO's 2023 Competency Framework: 'Drives change, inspires and empowers others in implementing change and offers guidance at all levels.' Carries the leadership content formerly assessed as the Leadership competency. | epso-cf-leaflet |
| Leadership (Civil Service Behaviour) | One of the nine Civil Service Behaviours: 'Show pride and passion for public service. Create and engage others in delivering a shared vision. Value difference, diversity and inclusion, ensuring fairness and opportunity for all.' | govuk-sp-behaviours |
| Leadership (EPSO, legacy) | Pre-2023 EPSO general competency, 'Manage, develop and motivate people to achieve results', marked 'for administrators only'. Superseded by Intrapreneurship in the 2023 framework - do not cite as current. | eurlex-epso-general-rules-2015 |
| Leading without authority | This knowledge base's editorial term for demonstrating leadership behaviours towards people who do not report to you. Not an official framework term, though the 2024 police CVF states supporting and inspiring is not restricted to formal management positions. | - |
| Managing a Quality Service | UK Civil Service Success Profiles behaviour: 'Deliver service objectives with professional excellence, expertise and efficiency, taking account of diverse customer needs.' Scored separately from Delivering at Pace. | govuk-sp-behaviours |
| Meaningful human involvement | Under UK GDPR article 22A, involvement such that a human can exercise real influence over a decision before it is applied and has the authority, discretion and competence to alter it; its absence makes a decision solely automated. | ico-rewired-meaningful-human-involvement |
| NHS Wales core values | The all-Wales core values in the NHS Wales Values and Standards of Behaviour Framework: quality and safety first; integrating improvement; prevention; working in true partnerships; investing in staff. | nhs-wales-values-nwssp |
| NHSScotland values | The four shared values of Scotland's health service — care and compassion; dignity and respect; openness, honesty and responsibility; quality and teamwork — set out in the 2020 Workforce Vision 'Everyone Matters', distinct from the six NHS Constitution values used in NHS England. | nhs-scot-everyone-matters |
| NICS Competency Framework | The April 2014 Northern Ireland Civil Service framework: ten competencies in three clusters (Setting Direction, Engaging People, Delivering Results) at six cumulative levels. NICS is a SEPARATE civil service from the Home Civil Service and uses Competence Based Interviews, not Success Profiles. | nics-cf-2014 |
| Nolan Principles | The Seven Principles of Public Life - selflessness, integrity, objectivity, accountability, openness, honesty and leadership - first published 31 May 1995 by the Committee on Standards in Public Life. | govuk-nolan-principles |
| Notice of Competition | The official published document specifying a given EPSO competition's tests and requirements — per EPSO, the only official source of detailed test information for that competition. | epso-faq-3104 |
| Operational validity | The correlation between a selection procedure's scores and later job performance, corrected where appropriate for criterion unreliability and range restriction; the value expected in an unrestricted applicant pool. | sackett-2023-iop |
| Oral presentation (EPSO) | An individual analysis-and-presentation exercise in which the candidate develops a proposal on a fictitious work-related problem and presents it to at least two assessors; indicative pre-2023 duration 50 minutes. | epso-ac-brochure-ad244 |
| ORCE | Observe, Record, Classify, Evaluate — the linear assessor process converting observed behaviour into a competency rating. Each stage is carried out independently of other assessors; classification happens after the candidate has left, and each behaviour is classified once and never double-counted. | cop-vbr-selection-2018 |
| Order of merit | The ranked list produced by a Civil Service selection panel; the Recruitment Principles require the candidate recommended for appointment to be the one placed first in order of merit. | csc-recruitment-principles-2018 |
| Order of merit / panel (Ireland) | Successful Irish public-service candidates are placed on a panel in order of merit, from which vacancies are filled; a panel place is not a guarantee of appointment. The Irish equivalent of a reserve list. | pj-eo-booklet-2022 |
| Panel interview | An interview conducted by more than one interviewer at once, scoring the same candidate against the same criteria; ACAS advises more than one interviewer where possible to reduce discrimination risk. | acas-interviewing |
| Past-behaviour question (PBQ) | An interview question asking what a candidate did do in a real past situation. | levashina-2014-peps |
| Police Scotland CVF | Police Scotland's own Competency and Values Framework: six competencies in three clusters, three cumulative levels, and four values (Integrity, Fairness, Respect, Human Rights) from the Code of Ethics for Policing in Scotland. Adapted with permission from College of Policing material and retaining the pre-2024 structure England and Wales has since dropped. | ps-cvf |
| Preparation (think) time | The interval between an asynchronous interview question appearing on screen and recording beginning, during which the candidate may plan but not record. | kpmg-application-process |
| Prioritising and organising | One of the eight general competencies of EPSO's pre-2023 model, assessed in the situational competency-based interview. | eurlex-epso-ad-399-22 |
| Public Appointments Service (PAS / publicjobs) | Ireland's independent, centralised recruiter for the Civil and public service; candidate portal and trading name publicjobs.ie. | pj-eo-booklet-2022 |
| Qualifying disclosure | Under s.43B Employment Rights Act 1996, a disclosure of information which 'in the reasonable belief of the worker making the disclosure, is made in the public interest' and tends to show one of the listed categories of relevant failure. | era-1996-s43b |
| Range restriction | Under-representation of the full ability range in a validation sample, which depresses an observed validity correlation; over-correcting for it inflates validity estimates — the core error identified in the 2022 re-analysis. | sackett-2022-jap |
| Reasonable accommodation (EU) | The EU-law equivalent of a reasonable adjustment: appropriate measures, where needed in a particular case, to enable a person with a disability to access, participate in or advance in employment, unless a disproportionate burden. EPSO's term is 'Reasonable Accommodations'. | eurlex-2000-78 |
| Reconciliation (panel scoring) | The step combining panellists' independent scores into one score per candidate. Cabinet Office guidance for delegated grades requires reconciliation by arithmetic average, with marked variances first discussed against the underlying evidence. | co-sp-delegated-grades-2020 |
| Recovery arc | This knowledge base's coaching term (not a framework term) for the structure of a strong setback answer: the setback is real and costly, the response is deliberate, and the ending is a durable change in how the candidate works. | - |
| Reserve list | Database of the successful candidates of an EPSO selection procedure (profile, competencies, availability), from which EU institutions recruit; listing is eligibility, not employment. | epso-faq-reserve-list |
| Resilience (EPSO, pre-2023) | A standalone general competency in EPSO's pre-2023 assessment-centre model, assessed in the SCBI. Superseded by Self-management in the 2023 framework. | epso-scbi-365-370 |
| Retake | A permitted re-recording of an answer in an asynchronous video interview, replacing the previous attempt; availability and count are set by the employer or platform, and may be a single attempt only. | targetjobs-video-interview-tips |
| Role actor | A trained person who plays a character (customer, colleague, member of the public) in a role-play exercise, observed by a separate assessor. | cop-vbr-selection-2018 |
| SCBI | Situational competency-based interview — an EPSO interview that assesses general competencies by asking the candidate how they would react in a specific situation, anchored to a written assignment. | epso-scbi |
| Section 75 (Northern Ireland Act 1998) | Statutory duty on NI public authorities to have due regard to promoting equality of opportunity across nine grounds, plus a distinct duty to promote good relations between persons of different religious belief, political opinion or racial group. Distinct from the Equality Act 2010 PSED. | ni-act-1998-s75 |
| Selection Board (EU) | The EU-institution equivalent of an interview panel, constituted under Annex III of the Staff Regulations: a chairman designated by the Appointing Authority plus equal numbers of Appointing-Authority and Staff-Committee members; boards of more than four members must include at least two members of each gender, and proceedings are secret. | eurlex-staff-regulations |
| Self-management (EPSO 2023) | The EPSO general competency carrying the resilience content since 2023: 'Organises own tasks, demonstrates responsibility and a constructive attitude at all times', with anchors on responding calmly to obstacles and adapting to changing work conditions. | epso-cf-leaflet |
| Situational question (SQ) | An interview question asking what a candidate would do in a hypothetical scenario, as distinct from a past-behaviour question. | levashina-2014-peps |
| SOAR | Situation, Obstacle, Action, Result — a practitioner variant of STAR replacing Task with Obstacle to emphasise problem-solving; not named in official UK/EU guidance reviewed. | interviewguys-soar |
| STAR | Situation, Task, Action, Result — the standard structure for evidencing a competency example; recommended in UK Civil Service candidate guidance and by the National Careers Service. | ncs-star-method |
| STARR / STAR(R) | STAR plus a final Reflection element on what was learned or would be done differently; documented by the University of Reading, Arden University and graduate employers via targetjobs. | arden-starr |
| Strength (Success Profiles) | In Success Profiles, 'the things that we do regularly, do well and that motivate us'. Strengths are not defined by job grade, and candidates are told NOT to rehearse answers because assessors seek an initial response - unlike Behaviours. | govuk-sp-strengths |
| Strengths-based interview | An interview probing what the candidate does regularly, does well and finds energising, expecting unrehearsed responses, as distinct from a competency/behaviour interview seeking prepared examples of past performance. | govuk-sp-strengths |
| Structured interview | An interview using a predefined set of questions, asked in the same order to all candidates, with responses scored against pre-agreed objective criteria, making responses comparable and reducing bias. | cipd-selection-methods |
| Success Profiles | The Civil Service recruitment framework: a per-vacancy bundle of up to five assessed elements — Behaviours, Strengths, Ability, Experience, Technical. | govuk-success-profiles |
| Telephone screening | An early-stage interview by phone used to filter a large applicant pool before later-stage interviews or assessment centres. | prospects-telephone |
| Time-factored scoring | Scoring in which the time taken to respond forms part of the performance score for an assessed area, as published by Deloitte UK for its immersive online assessment. | deloitte-early-careers-assessment |
| Unassigned-role (leaderless) group exercise | A group task with no designated leader in which candidates typically hold different briefing documents and must reach a joint decision through negotiation and compromise. | kent-assessment-centre-tasks |
| Values-based interview (VBI) | A structured interview focused on a candidate's values, distinguished by probing follow-up questions designed to reveal what the candidate considers important (HEE VBR Framework definition). | hee-vbr-framework-2016 |
| Values-based recruitment (VBR) | The NHS approach of recruiting students, trainees and employees on the basis that their individual values and behaviours align with the values of the NHS Constitution. | hee-vbr-page |
| We are emotionally aware | One of six competencies in the College of Policing CVF (2024); carries the resilience and wellbeing content, including managing one's own emotions to remain effective under pressure and seeking out support. | cop-cvf-2024-framework |
| We collaborate | One of six competencies in the College of Policing CVF (2024), described at three levels; covers working with colleagues, other organisations and the public. | cop-cvf-2024-framework |
| We support and inspire | One of six competencies in the 2024 police CVF, covering applying organisational values to provide inspiration and clarity. Explicitly 'not restricted to those who are in formal or senior management positions'. | cop-cvf-2024-framework |
| We take ownership | One of six competencies in the College of Policing CVF (2024); carries persistence and accountability content, including promoting a culture that responds to mistakes with learning rather than blame. | cop-cvf-2024-framework |
| Welsh Language Standards | Legally enforceable requirements on Welsh public bodies under the Welsh Language (Wales) Measure 2011, including duties to categorise each post's Welsh-language requirement, advertise qualifying posts in Welsh, and not treat Welsh applicants less favourably. | welsh-language-measure-2011 |
| Work sample test | An exercise letting assessors see a candidate in action in an activity representing a sample of the role, as distinct from an interview in which the candidate talks about past or hypothetical action. | cop-vbr-selection-2018 |
| Working Together | UK Civil Service Success Profiles behaviour: 'Form effective partnerships and relationships with people both internally and externally, from a range of diverse backgrounds, sharing information, resources and support.' | govuk-sp-behaviours |
| Working together (EPSO) | EPSO general competency: 'Demonstrates cross-functional cooperation and fosters diversity and inclusion. Cooperates well by seeking compromise, sharing knowledge and assisting others.' Decomposed into five observable anchors. | epso-cf-leaflet |
